Port State:
 - Forwarding
 - Blocking
 - Listening
 - Learning
 - Disabled
 - Shutdown
 - Not Present
 - Lower Layer Down

Descriptions:
 - Forwarding: The port is actively sending and receiving data packets.
 - Blocking: The port is not forwarding data packets to prevent loops in the network.
 - Listening: The port is in the process of determining the network topology and is not forwarding data packets.
 - Learning: The port is learning MAC addresses but is not yet forwarding data packets.
 - Disabled: The port is administratively disabled and does not participate in network traffic.
 - Shutdown: The port is administratively shut down and is not operational.
 - Not Present: The port is not physically present or connected.
 - Lower Layer Down: The port is down due to a failure in the lower layer, such as a physical connection issue.

Usage:
These port states are commonly used in networking devices such as switches to manage and control the flow of data within a network, ensuring efficient and loop-free communication.
Examples:
 - A switch port in the "Forwarding" state allows data to pass through normally.
 - A port in the "Blocking" state prevents data transmission to avoid network loops.
 - During network topology changes, a port may enter the "Listening" or "Learning" states before transitioning to "Forwarding."
