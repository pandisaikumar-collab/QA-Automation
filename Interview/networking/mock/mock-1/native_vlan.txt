a) what is native VLAN:

 - it is responsible for untagged traffic on a trunk port
 - by default, native VLAN is VLAN 1 on most switches
 - any untagged frames received on a trunk port are assigned to the native VLAN
 - it helps in backward compatibility with devices that do not support VLAN tagging
 - native VLAN should be the same on both ends of a trunk link to avoid VLAN mismatches

b) why is it important:
 - ensures untagged traffic is properly handled on trunk links
 - prevents potential security risks from untagged traffic being misrouted
 - helps maintain network segmentation and organization
 - important for interoperability with legacy devices
