q) difference between tagged and untagged traffic:

 - Tagged Traffic:
   - Contains VLAN ID in the Ethernet frame header (802.1Q tagging).
   - Used on trunk ports to carry traffic for multiple VLANs between switches.
   - Allows switches to identify which VLAN the traffic belongs to.
- Example: A frame with VLAN ID 10 is tagged as it travels across a trunk
    link between switches.


- Untagged Traffic:
    - Does not contain VLAN ID in the Ethernet frame header.
    - Used on access ports where devices are connected to a single VLAN.
    - The switch assigns the traffic to the appropriate VLAN based on the port configuration.

- Example: A PC connected to an access port for VLAN 20 sends untagged traffic,
    and the switch assigns it to VLAN 20.
