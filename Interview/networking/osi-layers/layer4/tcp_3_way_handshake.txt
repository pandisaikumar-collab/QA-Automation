> A process to start a TCP connection using:
 1. SYN (synchronize)
 2. SYN-ACK (synchronize-acknowledge)
 3. ACK (acknowledge)

> The three-way handshake is used to establish a reliable connection between a client and server before data transmission begins.
> Steps involved in the TCP three-way handshake:
 1. SYN: (request to start a connection)
    - The client sends a TCP segment with the SYN flag set to the server.
    - This segment includes an initial sequence number (ISN) chosen by the client.
 2. SYN-ACK: (acknowledgment of the request))
    - The server responds with a TCP segment that has both the SYN and ACK flags set.
    - The ACK acknowledges the client's SYN by incrementing the client's ISN by 1.
    - The server also chooses its own ISN and includes it in the SYN-ACK segment.
 3. ACK: (final acknowledgment)
    - The client sends a final TCP segment with the ACK flag set.
    - This ACK acknowledges the server's SYN by incrementing the server's ISN by 1.

> After these three steps, a reliable TCP connection is established, and data transmission can begin.
