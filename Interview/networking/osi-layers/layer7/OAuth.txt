1. Secure authentication method
2. Allows third-party applications to access user data without sharing credentials
3. Uses tokens for access delegation
4. Commonly used in web applications and APIs
5. Supports various grant types (authorization code, implicit, client credentials, etc.)
6. Enhances security by limiting the scope and duration of access
7. Widely adopted by major platforms (Google, Facebook, Twitter, etc.)

Example: Login Google using OAuth to allow a third-party app to access your email without sharing your Google password.
Keyword: Token-based authentication
