q) what is trunking:

 - trunking is the process of carrying multiple VLANs over a single
   physical link between network devices, such as switches or routers.

Example:
  - Switch A and Switch B are connected via a trunk link.
  - Switch A has VLANs 10, 20, and 30 configured.
  - Switch B also has VLANs 10, 20, and 30 configured.
  - The trunk link allows traffic from all three VLANs to pass between
    the switches.
  - This enables devices in the same VLAN on different switches to communicate
    with each other.


Topology Diagram:
       +-------------------+          +-------------------+
       |     Switch A      |          |     Switch B      |
       +-------------------+          +-------------------+
       | Port 1  | VLAN 10 |----+     | Port 1  | VLAN 10 |----+
       | Port 2  | VLAN 20 |----+     | Port 2  | VLAN 20 |----+
       | Port 3  | VLAN 30 |----+     | Port 3  | VLAN 30 |----+
       | Port 4  | Trunk   |==========| Port 4  | Trunk   |==========|
       +-------------------+          +-------------------+
Legend:
    - Devices in the same VLAN (e.g., VLAN 10 on Switch A and Switch B)
    can communicate with each other.
    - The trunk link carries traffic for all VLANs configured on both switches.
    - Trunking allows for efficient use of physical links while maintaining
    VLAN segregation across multiple switches.
