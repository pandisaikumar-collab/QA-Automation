q) difference between access port and trunk port:

  - Access Port:
    - Connects end devices (PCs, printers) to the switch.
    - Carries traffic for a single VLAN.
    - Tags are removed before sending data to the device.
    - Example: A PC in the HR department connected to VLAN 10.

  - Trunk Port:
    - Connects switches or network devices.
    - Carries traffic for multiple VLANs simultaneously.
    - Tags are preserved to identify VLANs.
    - Example: A link between two switches carrying VLANs 10, 20, and 30.
