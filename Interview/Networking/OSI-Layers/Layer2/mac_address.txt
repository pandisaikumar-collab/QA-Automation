> Mac address (Media access control address):
 - A physical address of a network device.
 - It is a unique identifier assigned to network interfaces for communications on the physical network segment.
 - Mac addresses are typically assigned by the manufacturer of the network interface card (NIC) and are
    stored in the hardware.
  - A Mac address is a 48-bit address usually represented as six groups of two hexadecimal digits,
    separated by colons (:) or hyphens (-). For example, 00:1A:2B:3C:4D:5E.
   - Mac addresses operate at the Data Link Layer (Layer 2) of the OSI model
